Protocol Overview

How Harmonix is structured and why it's built this way.

Four Problems We Solve

1. Capital Isolation

In most DeFi systems, capital is siloed by product. Harmonix enables a single deposit to back funding strategies, lending, and ecosystem yield simultaneously — all while continuing to accrue base returns.

2. Static Yield Strategies

Most yield products rely on fixed allocations or single-protocol exposure. Harmonix uses dynamic strategies that adapt to funding rates, volatility, and ecosystem conditions in real time. When rates compress, capital rotates automatically.

3. Directional Risk

Traditional yield products embed hidden market exposure. Harmonix centers on market-neutral structures that remove price beta from stablecoin products, letting users earn yield without depending on asset appreciation.

⚠️
haHYPE and hakHYPE are not fully market-neutral. They carry directional exposure to HYPE price. Only the delta-neutral allocation within those vaults hedges the position — holders remain partially exposed to HYPE movement. See the haHYPE product page for details.

4. Disconnection from Execution

Harmonix is built directly on Hyperliquid's execution environment. Funding markets, lending, and trading activity feed user yield without bridges, external oracles, or lag.

Where Yield Comes From

All three ha-stablecoins share the same underlying yield engine. Capital is routed across three primary sources:

Funding income (Delta Neutral). Capital is paired long spot / short perpetuals on HyperCore to earn funding payments while eliminating price exposure. This is the primary, most consistent yield source.

Spot supply yield. A portion of capital is deployed into HyperEVM lending and liquidity protocols — Felix, Hyperswap, Kinetiq, and others — earning additional lending and LP yield on top of funding income.

Idle reserves. A buffer of undeployed capital is maintained at all times to support smooth redemptions without disrupting active strategy positions.

⚠️
ha-stablecoins are not guaranteed fixed-yield instruments. Yield fluctuates with funding rates, market conditions, and protocol activity. The $1 peg is maintained through market-neutral strategy design, not a hard redemption guarantee.

Yield-Bearing Tokens

haHYPE and hakHYPE are ERC-4626 yield-bearing tokens that put HYPE and kHYPE to work across HyperEVM. Yield accrues automatically into the share price — no claiming, no staking step required.

⚠️
haHYPE and hakHYPE are not delta-neutral products. The overall position carries directional HYPE exposure. While a delta-neutral sub-strategy within the vault reduces net beta, holders participate in HYPE price movements on the non-hedged portion. These are yield-enhancing tokens, not market-neutral instruments.

haHYPE

haHYPE is an ERC-4626 yield-bearing token for HYPE depositors. When you deposit HYPE, Harmonix uses it as collateral to mint feUSD via Felix Protocol. Capital splits: 50% shorts HYPE perps on HyperCore earning 10–15% APR from positive funding; 50% buys back HYPE and restakes it across HyperEVM — DEXes, money markets, Pendle, and Kinetiq — generating ecosystem yield and points simultaneously.

The two legs offset directional exposure. Both generate yield. Net delta is close to zero on the hedged portion — but the overall haHYPE product remains HYPE-exposed on the non-hedged allocation.

How It Works

cdharUSD is a yield-bearing stable position with a built-in HAR entry option attached. You earn haUSDC-equivalent yield throughout the term. At maturity, you choose: convert into HAR at your locked price if it's favorable, or redeem stablecoins plus yield. Nothing happens automatically — the decision is always yours.

1

Choose your term and deposit

Deposit USDC or USDH into the 1-, 2-, or 3-month vault. Receive cdharUSD (1/2/3) as your receipt token. Your HAR conversion price is locked the moment you deposit via the continuous tick auction.

2

Earn yield throughout the term

Capital is deployed into the same delta-neutral engine powering haUSDC. Yield compounds into your position continuously. No action required.

3

Decide at maturity

Convert to HAR if the market price exceeds your locked conversion price. Redeem stablecoins + yield for zero HAR exposure. Or exit early at any time with a small fee (conversion right forfeited).

Conversion Price Mechanics

At deposit, your HAR entry price is locked via a continuous tick-based auction. Each tick has a fixed token capacity — when it fills, the next tick opens 0.75% higher. Large deposits consume multiple ticks and receive a weighted average price. Depositing during quiet periods and splitting large amounts over time are the most direct ways to secure a better average entry price.

What Happens When You Convert

When you convert at maturity, your stablecoins move into the Harmonix treasury. The treasury uses those funds to acquire HYPE and deploy into Harmonix strategies — growing the long-term HYPE reserve. The yield from that HYPE reserve flows into the xHAR vault for staked HAR holders.

HAR

The native token of the Harmonix Protocol. Total supply: 1,000,000,000 HAR. No inflation.

Product Overview

HAR is the native token of the Harmonix Protocol, serving as the core mechanism for governance, economic growth, and user ownership. It is designed to drive governance decisions, treasury direction, and utility-driven participation across the ecosystem.

The total supply is fixed at 1,000,000,000 HAR. There is no inflation through additional minting.

This page explains what HAR is, how it is distributed, how xHAR works, and how value flows to HAR across the ecosystem. For details on how stablecoin conversions interact with HAR, see Convertible Deposits. For platform-wide risks, see Risks, Security & Audits.

What HAR Is and How It Fits

HAR exists to connect protocol activity to long-term value and give holders a direct voice in how Harmonix evolves. HAR holders propose and vote on protocol parameters, treasury allocations, emissions schedules, and the direction of Harmonix's HYPE treasury.

Across the product suite, HAR is the destination for users who want to move beyond yield and into ownership. Stable depositors in cdharUSD3 hold a locked right to convert into HAR at maturity. xHAR stakers earn protocol revenue from that conversion flow. HAR ties the two sides together — governance at the top, yield alignment underneath.

xHAR — Staked HAR

Stake HAR to receive xHAR. xHAR accumulates yield automatically from three sources: early-exit fees from cdharUSD3 holders who leave before maturity, yield generated on HYPE purchased with converted stablecoins, and additional protocol revenue.

You do not manage positions or track individual vaults. You hold xHAR, and when the system generates revenue the token appreciates. xHAR holders are the equity layer of Harmonix — when the treasury's HYPE stack grows, so does their yield.

The Flywheel

Stable users deposit into cdharUSD3 and earn yield. When they convert into HAR, their stablecoins move into the Harmonix treasury and are used to acquire HYPE. That HYPE generates real yield. That yield flows into the xHAR vault. A stronger xHAR makes HAR more attractive. A more attractive HAR draws more stable depositors. The loop restarts.

🔄
Stablecoin user: "I park stables, earn yield, and if Harmonix does well I can rotate into HAR at my locked price."

HAR staker: "I lock HAR and share the upside whenever stable holders convert and the treasury's HYPE stack grows."

Integration Guide

Depositing (ERC-4626)

All Harmonix vaults implement the standard ERC-4626 interface. To deposit:

  1. Call approve(vaultAddress, amount) on the underlying asset token
  2. Call deposit(assets, receiver) on the vault contract
  3. Receive ha-token shares proportional to current PPS

Withdrawing

In V2, withdrawals go through an on-chain queue:

  1. Call requestWithdraw(shares) — creates a pending withdrawal request
  2. Wait for processing (see withdrawal timing table in Deposits & Withdrawals)
  3. Call claimWithdraw(requestId) to receive underlying assets

Reading NAV / Price Per Share

Call pricePerShare() or convertToAssets(1e18) on any vault contract to get the current NAV-based exchange rate. This is updated by the automated NAV Oracle pipeline.

Proof of Solvency

Harmonix uses Accountable Capital for on-chain, verifiable proof that vault assets match liabilities in real time.

🟢
Proof of Solvency is provided by Accountable Capital ↗ — a Merkle tree-based attestation system that allows any holder to verify their position is fully backed at any point in time.

What is Proof of Solvency?

Accountable Capital publishes a Merkle tree of all vault positions and corresponding liabilities. Any user can cryptographically verify that their share of the vault is backed by real assets — without trusting a centralized report. The proof is generated on-chain via ForDeFi → Merkle Tree, as shown in the V1 vault architecture diagram.

FAQ

Common questions about Harmonix products and architecture.

Products

Is haUSDC delta-neutral?

Yes. haUSDC is fully market-neutral — capital is deployed into delta-neutral strategies that eliminate directional price exposure. The $1 peg is maintained through the hedged structure, not a redemption guarantee.

Are haHYPE and hakHYPE delta-neutral?

No. haHYPE and hakHYPE are not fully delta-neutral products. A sub-strategy within each vault runs a delta-neutral position to reduce net beta, but the overall product carries directional HYPE exposure. If HYPE price changes, your position value changes accordingly on the non-hedged portion.

What is the difference between cdharUSD1, cdharUSD2, and cdharUSD3?

Only the lock term differs. cdharUSD1 = 1-month lock, cdharUSD2 = 2-month lock, cdharUSD3 = 3-month lock. All three use the same yield engine and HAR conversion mechanic.

Do I have to convert into HAR at cdharUSD maturity?

No. Conversion is always optional. If HAR is trading below your locked conversion price, the right has no value and you simply redeem stablecoins plus yield — identical to holding haUSDC for the term.

Does yield pay out automatically?

Yes. Yield accrues into the share price continuously. There is no claim step — holding the ha-token is sufficient. When you redeem, you receive more of the underlying than you deposited.

Why are haHYPE withdrawals in WHYPE and not HYPE?

WHYPE is the HyperEVM-native wrapped form of HYPE. Native HYPE withdrawal support is on the roadmap for a future update.

Architecture

What is HIP-3?

HIP-3 is Hyperliquid's native standard for yield-bearing spot tokens. Harmonix uses three HIP-3 markets — Felix (feUSD), Ondo (USDY), and Hyna (sUSDe) — as strategy inputs. These tokens trade natively on HyperCore spot markets, enabling Harmonix to earn their underlying yield without bridges.

What oracle does Harmonix use?

haHYPE and hakHYPE use Pyth Network for NAV pricing and collateral valuation. haUSDC is evaluating Pyth or Chainlink for V2 — decision TBD at launch.

Is Harmonix audited?

Yes. Contracts are audited by Verichains, Shieldify, and Zenith. Audit reports will be published at V2 mainnet launch.
